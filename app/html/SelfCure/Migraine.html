Description -
A migraine is a severe, painful headache that is often preceded or accompanied by sensory warning signs such as flashes of light, blind spots, tingling in the arms and legs, nausea, vomiting, and increased sensitivity to light and sound. The excruciating pain that migraines bring can last for hours or even days. Migraines are about three times more common in women than in men.

Symptoms -

  • Pain on one side of the head and often starts from behind the eye
  • Pulsating or throbbing pain, nausea and vomiting. You can feel the blood pulsating through the affected side of the head
  • Changes in vision such as seeing flashes of light, blind spots or blurry vision
  • Strong and painful reactions to light and loud noises
  • The simple act of moving may be difficult during the migraine attack. Pain may worsen from activity
  • Numbness and weakness or an arm or a leg. In some cases the entire face and tongue may get numb making the speech slurry
  • Digestive problems are experienced sometimes as the headache develops
  • Irritability, Laziness, Stiff Neck, Stuffy Nose, Drowsiness, Constipation
  • Confused thinking, Difficulty in concentrating

Causes of Migraine -

  • Dehydration, Skipping meals or fasting
  • Fatigue, Stress, Tension, Depression or Anxiety
  • Intense physical activity
  • Changes in the physical environment such as changes in season, temperature or altitude
  • Various sensory stimuli such as bright lights, flashing lights, unusual smells and loud noise
  • Changes in sleeping patterns, sleep disorders or lack of sleep
  • Poor posture, Neck or Shoulder tension
  • Alcohol and Tobacco smoke
  • Certain foods like Chocolate, Cheese, Citrus fruits, Alcohol, Beans, Nuts, Breads and other baked foods, raisins, Caffeine etc
  • Hormonal changes (many women experience migraines during their menstrual cycle, menopause or pregnancy)
  • Several prescription and non-prescription medications can aggravate migraines

How to avoid -

  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet and do not skip meals
  • Exercise regularly. Exercise can help to reduce tension and stress and lower the chances of migraines
  • Try stick to a regular sleeping pattern, and ensure that you get enough sleep each night
  • Learn to relax. Practice relaxation techniques and deep breathing (Yoga)
  • Avoid tobacco smoke and limit your caffeine intake
  • Avoid direct Sunlight
  • Keep a migraine journal to help identify migraine triggers. Include all the foods, activities or anything else you did that day. Once you know your triggers then you can do your best to avoid them
  • If you are a woman suffering from regular migraines, it may be useful to limit your estrogen intake (birth control pills or hormone replacement therapy)
