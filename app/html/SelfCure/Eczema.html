Description -
Eczema also called as dermatitis, is a group of skin disorders which can affect all age groups. The severity of this disease can vary. In more than 90% of cases, eczema is found to happen in children below 5 years of age. It is a non-contagious ailment.

Symptoms -
  • Itching
  • Redness on skin
  • Dry and flaky skin
  • Itchy blisters
  • Inflammation on skin
  • Small bumps on forehead, neck and cheek
  • Rough and thickened skin.

Causes -

  • Eczema is measured to be hereditary. Studies reveal that a person is prone to eczema if his family has a history of directly linked conditions like hay fever and asthma.
  • Irritants like smoke, chemicals, detergents, solvents and so on, could aggravate eczema. Even weather circumstances could aggravate the condition. Extreme stress, heat and emotional stress also aggravates the symptoms of eczema.
  • Some time blood circulation problem in the leg can also cause eczema.
  • Lack of vitamin B6 causes eczema.

How to avoid -

  • Prevent dry skin by taking warm (not hot) showers rather than baths. Use a mild soap or body cleanser. Dry yourself very carefully and apply moisturizing skin lotions all over your body. Avoid lotions with fragrances or other irritating substances.
  • Avoid wearing tight-fitting, rough, or scratchy clothing.
  • Avoid scratching the rash. If you can't stop yourself from scratching, cover the area with a dressing. Wear gloves at night to minimize skin damage from scratching.
  • Anything that causes sweating can irritate the rash. Avoid strenuous exercise during a flare.

  • If you are allergic or susceptible to certain foods then try avoiding them, because they can trigger eczema. Dairy products, eggs, nuts, preservatives and citrus fruits are more expected to trigger eczema.
  • Forever avoid substances you are allergic to.
  • Wear mostly cotton clothes, as they do not irritate your itchy skin. Stay away from wool clothes and blankets.
  • Take light diet.
  • Avoid cutting of vegetables especially garlic, onion, tomatoes, and ladies finger. Also avoid peeling of oranges.
  • The other offending substances are rubber footwear, spectacle frames, furs, artificial jewelry, and synthetic clothes.
