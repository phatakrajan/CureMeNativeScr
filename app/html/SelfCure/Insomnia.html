Description - The term insomnia literally denotes a complete lack of sleep. It is, however, used to indicate a relative inability to sleep that consists of difficulty in falling asleep, difficulty in remaining asleep, early final awakening, or combinations of these complaints. This disease has assumed alarming proportions in the present times, especially among the upper classes in the urban set-up.

Symptoms -

  • Difficulty falling asleep,  Sleeplessness
  • Waking up often at night
  • Difficulty returning to sleep after awakening in the middle of the night
  • Waking too early 
  • Feeling that you haven’t slept at all, the next day, Drowsiness, Tiredness
  • Racing mind; which causes people to keep shifting from being depressed to being over-excited and hyperactive
  • Physical and Mental Fatigue
  • Difficulty in concentrating, Lapses of memory, loss of coordination, Irritability
  • Headaches and Migraines

Causes of Insomnia -

  • Feelings like depression, anxiety and stress may affect a person’s ability to fall asleep
  • Suppressed thoughts of resentment, anger, and bitterness
  • Constipation, Overeating at night, going to bed hungry
  • Too much intake of tea, caffeine or other stimulants
  • Smoking
  • Bladder ailments
  • Hormonal imbalance
  • Magnesium or iron deficiency
  • Jet lag
  • A side effect of some prescription medication (especially anti-depressant medication)

How to avoid -

  • Make your bedroom an inviting place. Keep the room free of clutter and distractions.
  • Be sure you have the right bed and mattress for your needs, dark curtains to block light, earplugs if night noises disturb you
  • Use the bed only for sleeping
  • Establish a regular sleep-wake cycle
  • Reduce sleeping hours. Too much sleep can also cause insomnia.
  • Curb the nap habit. Try to avoid naps during the day
  • Limit your consumption of caffeine and other stimulants in the afternoon and evening
  • Watch your alcohol intake
  • Fit in some exercise during the day, but don't exercise strenuously right before bedtime
  • Eat light meals in the evening
