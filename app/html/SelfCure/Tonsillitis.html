Description - Tonsillitis refers to acute inflammation of the tonsils, which lie, one on each side of the throat. The tonsils are part of the immune system and protect against germs by producing antibodies. These antibodies play an important role in the body’s immune system by attacking bacteria and viruses. However, during this process of protecting the body, the tonsils may also become inflamed or infected.

Symptoms -

  • Mild or severe sore throat that may develop gradually or suddenly
  • Pain and Difficulty in swallowing food or your own saliva
  • Fever, vomiting
  • Enlarged, tender glands in the jaw and neck
  • Loss of voice
  • Headache, Earache, General aches and pain
  • There is also likely to be some accumulation of pus or a pasty and foul smelling infected material on the pits of the tonsils, Foul smelling breath.

Causes of Tonsillitis -

  • A prolonged sore throat caused by an infection could at times develop into tonsillitis
  • Constant dry cough
  • Forcible and constant use of voice - often at a high pitch
  • Chronic Congestion, blockade of nose
  • Excessive use of spices, condiments, acrid, pungent and astringent food items
  • Excessive eating of toffees, sweets, tamarind, tartaric acid, chewing gum etc.

How to avoid -

  • Wash your hands thoroughly with warm water and soap to prevent the spread of germs
  • Cough and sneeze into a tissue to prevent germs from spreading
  • Eat healthy, balanced meals that include all the food groups
  • Drink plenty of water to rid the body’s system of unwanted toxins and detox every once in a while
  • Do not share personal items such as toothbrushes, drinking glasses or eating utensils with others
  • Avoid close contact with those who are infected with tonsillitis
  • Increase your intake of vitamins to strengthen your immune system
  • Don’t smoke, as this increases the risk of developing tonsillitis
