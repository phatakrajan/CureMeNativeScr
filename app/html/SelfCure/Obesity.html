Description -
Obesity is a physical condition that occurs from excessive storage of fat in the body. Obesity has been defined as a weight more than 20% above what is measured usual as per the body mass index, which is calculated from an individual's age, height, and weight. Excess of fatty tissues results in obesity and causes fitness disorders. One of the places where the excessive body fat can easily be seen is around the waistline. A waist measurement of 40-45 inches or more in men and 35 inches or more in women is linked to an amplified risk for heart disease and other health problems associated with obesity. 

Symptoms -

  • High blood pressure
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Gallbladder disease
  • Some types of cancer
  • High cholesterol
  • Sleeping and breathing problems
  • Shortness of breath with exertion
  • Depression or other emotional problems
  • Difficulty in walking or moving around
  • Heartburn
  • Rashes in the folds of the skin
  • Snoring

Causes of Obesity -

  • Overeating and an Inactive or Sedentary lifestyle 
  • Genetic disorders 
  • Binge eating disorder, little exercise and crash diets all slow down the metabolism and make weight loss difficult
  • Weight cycling, caused by repeated attempts to lose weight by dieting 
  • Some people overeat because of stress, depression, hopelessness, anger, boredom, and many other reasons that have nothing to do with hunger
  • Insufficient sleep 
  • Underlying illness such as hypothyroidism
  • Certain medications such as atypical antipsychotics
  • Smoking cessation 
  • While weight gain during pregnancy is expected and essential for a healthy baby, many women struggle to lose weight after childbirth, commonly referred to as 'pregnancy weight'

How to avoid -

  • Limit the intake of fried foods which are rich in fat. Instead, broil or bake your food
  • Limit meat intake to once every day. Prefer to eat poultry and fish over other meat items
  • Eat enough portions from all major food groups to ensure your diet is rich in nutrition
  • Do not keep any stock of junk food in your home
  • If you do not feel hungry at regular meal time, then cut down your portion size and eat just enough food to keep you satiated and energetic for your daily tasks
  • Reduce the consumption of monosodium glutamate (MSG) and salt
  • Cut down on the sugar
  • Drink plenty of water every day to flush out toxins and impurities from your system
  • Do not consume more than four eggs a week as these are high in cholesterol
  • Limit intake of desserts and similar high-calorie items which are low in nutrition
  • Drink alcohol in moderation or avoid it altogether
  • Do at least 30 minutes of moderate physical activity every day, practice yoga, meditation or other self-awareness techniques to improve your self-control
