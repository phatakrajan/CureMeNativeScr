Description -
Vertigo is a sensation that everything around you is spinning or moving, which is usually caused by a problem in the inner ear, but can also be caused by vision problems. People with vertigo commonly feel things are moving when they are standing completely still and everything around them is still. Vertigo is more severe than dizziness. People with vertigo may find it harder to move around because the spinning sensation tends to affect balance.

Symptoms -

  • A sensation that everything around you is moving or spinning
  • Abnormal movement in the eye 
  • Loss of balance, Lightheadedness
  • Blurred vision and other vision problems 
  • Walking problems, Problem in standing still properly
  • Hearing loss and ringing sensation 
  • Nausea and vomiting 
  • Perspiration or sweating 
  • Difficulties in speaking normally
  • Reduction in the levels of consciousness or complete loss of consciousness 
  • Weakness
 

Causes of Vertigo -

  • The sensation of motion initiated by sudden head movements or moving the head in a certain direction, Neck Injury
  • Inflammation within the inner ear
  • Fluctuating pressure of inner ear fluid
  • Decreased blood flow to the base of the brain
  • Migraine
  • Motion Sickness
  • Vertigo may also occur as a side effect of some types of medication

How to avoid -

  • Perform eye exercises. These include looking up and down, side-to-side, and from near to far. Begin slowly and gradually build up speed
  • Move your head to the front and back and turn it from side to side beginning slowly and building up speed
  • Change body positions. Move from sitting to standing
  • Therapeutic exercises
  • Avoid fast or sudden body movements
  • Do not exercise with full stomach

Self Help -

  • Keep still, don't try to read, and avoid bright lights. Slowly resume normal activity after an attack to allow your body to gradually adjust to movement again
  • Breathe deeply and slowly
  • Take small sips of water
  • Soak 1 tsp of Gooseberry powder along with 1 tsp Coriander seeds. Leave over night. Strain and add 1/2 tsp Sugar and drink
  • Take Ginger or Ginger tea
  • Eat a lot of fresh fruits and vegetables, Eat celery/celery leaves
  • Eat a low-salt diet
  • Avoid Sugary foods, Aged Meats, Nuts, Seeds, Cheese, Fried foods
  • Eliminate alcohol from the diet completely 
  • Quit smoking
