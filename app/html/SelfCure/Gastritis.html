Description -
Gastritis is an inflammation, irritation, or erosion of the lining of the stomach. The inflammatory lesions may be either acute (suddenly) or chronic (gradually). Gastritis is actually the medical term for almost any stomach upset, sometimes also referred to as gastroenteritis. If gastritis is left untreated, it can lead to a severe loss in blood, or in some cases increase the risk of developing stomach cancer.

Symptoms -

  • Nausea, Vomiting, Headache, recurrent upset stomach, gas, fever
  • Abdominal bloating; a general feeling of uneasiness, Ache and discomfort in the region of the stomach
  • Burning or gnawing feeling in the stomach between meals or at night
  • Indigestion, Loss of appetite
  • Black, tarry stools
  • Additional symptoms are a coated tongue, foul breath, bad taste in the mouth, increased flow of saliva, scanty urination and mental depression.
  • In supplementary chronic cases the patient complaints of heartburn and a feeling of fullness in the abdomen, particularly after meals.

Causes of Gastritis -

  • Digestive disorders are the main cause of gastritis troubles.
  • Habitual overeating; eating of poorly combined or improperly cooked food.
  • Excessive intake of strong tea, coffee, or alcoholic drinks or usual use of large quantities of condiments and sauces.
  • Worry, anxiety, grief, and prolonged tension, strong acids, and caustic materials.
  • Regular use of pain killers and non steroidal anti inflammatory drugs.

How to avoid -

  • Avoid excessive use of alcohol and drink only in moderation as alcohol irritates the stomach lining
  • Eliminate indigestion by eating several small meals each day rather than 2 or 3 large ones. This helps prevent acid from upsetting your stomach
  • Stay away from foods that upset your stomach, especially spicy or deep fried foods
  • Quit smoking and avoid secondhand smoke. Smoking irritates the stomach lining making you more susceptible to gastritis and ulcers
  • Avoid regular consumption of pain killers.
  • Get yourself down to a healthy weight.
  • Decrease the stress in your life as much as you can. Stress increases stomach acids and slow down the digestive process. Find some new ways to relax and unwind like walking or yoga.
