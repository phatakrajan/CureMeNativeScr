Description -
A nosebleed can be a rather frightening experience, not just for children, but also for adults. However, even though it may not seem so, nosebleeds are rarely serious and can be managed easily. They are fairly common in children, as well as grownups.

Symptoms -

  • The appearance of blood from either one or both the nostrils 
  • The necessity to swallow frequently, because of a blocked feeling in the nasal passages 
  • The sensation of a fluid at the back of the nose and the throat 
  • Difficulties in breathing, where you may feel that you are choking a bit
  • Appearance of a nasal deformity 
  • Bleeding in the gums 
  • Disturbances in vision 
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness 
  • Facial pain, accompanied by numbness in the area 
  • Fainting spells 
  • Hearing problems 
  • High fever and chills 
  • Seizures 
  • Swelling around the nose or the cheeks 
  • Vomiting, especially if there are traces of blood in it 

Causes of Nose Bleeding -

  • Nasal or sinus infections 
  • Foreign objects stuck in the nasal passages 
  • Infections in the nose and nasal passages 
  • Nasal surgery
  • Exposure to dry and warm air for a long period of time 
  • Sneezing excessively or blowing the nose too vigorously 
  • Perforation or deviation in the nasal septum 
  • Use of certain medications or illegal drugs like cocaine 
  • Presence of kidney diseases or liver diseases 
  • Vascular formations in the nose 
  • Tumors in the nasal passages 
  • High blood pressure, accompanied by excess levels of anxiety 
  • Clotting disorders that may run in the family 
  • Leukemia 
  • Exposure to chemical irritants like ammonia 
  • Common colds 
  • Excessive use of nasal sprays 
  • Alcohol abuse 
  • Nasal polyps 
  • Atherosclerosis, which is a disease of the arteries 
  • Exposure to sudden altitude changes, as when flying

How to avoid:-

  • If you are prone to nosebleeds, check the humidity in your house, and increase it to the normal range
  • Keep nostrils moist. Take a shower and breathe deeply to get moisture into your nose. Then put petroleum jelly on the inside of the nose to keep it moist. A saline solution spray will also help
  • Daily consumption of citrus fruit can help eliminate recurring nosebleeds.
  • Make sure you do not have high blood pressure
  • Avoid foods that may give you a severe allergic reaction
  • Putting a few drops of castor oil, vitamin E or zinc oxide in your nose each day will prevent recurring nosebleeds
  • Don't pick your nose with sharp objects

Self Help:-

  • Apply an icepack or a few cubes of ice in a towel directly to the nose and use it for at least 5 minutes or so. Alternately, a bag of frozen vegetables will also do 
  • Putting wet cloth on the head is a good remedy to stop nose bleeding
  • It is best to keep your head elevated at least till the bleeding stops
  • Dip a small piece of cotton in a bit of white vinegar and plug it into the nostril that is bleeding
  • Putting a drop of Lemon juice in both the nostrils, helps to stop nose bleeding immediately
  • Put drops of juice of Neem or Basil or Onion or sweet grape or pomegranate in the nostrils stop nose bleeding
  • Make a solution with water and baking soda and spray 2 to 3 puffs of it on the nose
  • The juice squeezed out of Black Cotton leaves applied on the center of the head, stops nose bleeding 
  • Eating a piece of Coconut everyday on empty stomach, helps to overcome nose bleeding
  • Apply a paste of Black gram ground with water on the forehead
