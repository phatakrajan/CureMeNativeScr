Description - Stress is a situation where the individual feels some sort of mental or emotional pressure. Individuals are often unaware that they are under stress. Stress usually produces a physical and emotional response in your body. This is actually good for you as it works to stimulate your body into action. However, over time, this may prove to be harmful to the body as it will continuously be exposed to the stressful state which will eventually have an adverse effect on your health.

Symptoms -

  • Headache, Chest pain, Muscle tension or pain
  • Fatigue, Sleep problems
  • Anxiety, Restlessness, Lack of motivation or focus
  • Depression, Irritability, Anger, Emotional outbursts
  • Increased heart rate, Sweating and generally, Shortness of breath, Increased state of alertness
  • Unable to concentrate, Becoming easily confused, Feeling you can't cope
  • Having memory problems
  • Thinking about negative things all the time, Negative self-talk
  • Having marked mood swing
  • Finding it hard to make decisions
  • Overeating or Under-eating
  • Stomach upset
  • Drug or alcohol abuse, Tobacco use
  • Social withdrawal

Causes of Stress -

  • Financial Problems
  • Fear, Envy, Uncertainty
  • Workplace Stress
  • Personal Relationships
  • Health – Heart diseases, hypertension, problems with eye sight etc
  • Responsibility increase
  • Loud noises, Blinding light, Severe heat or cold, X-rays and other forms of radiation, Drugs, Chemicals, Various toxic substances

How to avoid -

  • Meditation, Yoga, Regular exercise
  • Eat healthy food and chew well. Don't eat just because you are bored, unhappy, or angry
  • Drink lot of water
  • Don’t smoke and consume alcohol
  • Find a hobby which has no deadlines, no pressures, and which can be picked up or left easily, takes the mind off stresses
  • Don't over commit yourself
  • Be responsible for your work. Do your best with every opportunity
  • Communicate with friends, Keep yourself happy

Self Help -

  • Slowly breath in and very slowly breathing out. If you do this a few times, and concentrate fully on breathing, you may find it quite relaxing
  • Chew twelve leaves of basil two times in a day, morning and evening, for preventing stress
  • A tea can be prepared by pouring a cup of boiling water over one teaspoon of dried out or fresh sage leaves. The water should be enclosed and infused for several minutes. It should then be strained and sweetened with honey, if preferred
  • Drink a glass of warm Milk with Honey and Cinnamon
  • Drink hot Water with a spoonful of honey in it
  • Smell Carom seeds
  • Eat yogurt, sprouts
  • Take a hot water bath
  • Caffeine in Coffee and a lot of Soft drinks, Salt, Sugar, Cigarettes, and Alcohol should be avoided
