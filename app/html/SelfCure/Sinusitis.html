Description -
Sinusitis refers to an inflammation of the mucus membrane lining the para-nasal sinuses. It often follows a common cold, influenza, and other general infections. Infecting germs sometimes find their way into sinuses or chambers on either side of the nasal passage, leading to sinus trouble. The sinuses are basically moist air passages that are hollowed out in the facial bones, behind and around the nasal area

Symptoms -

  • Pain or pressure in the forehead, eyes or cheeks
  • Post nasal drip
  • Stuffy nose, Runny nose, Sneezing, difficulty in breathing
  • Fever, Headache
  • Lack of appetite
  • Sore throat
  • Aching in the upper teeth
  • A cold that seems to get better, then worsens

Causes of Sinusitis -

  • Excessive mucus production, resulting in blockage of the sinuses, which could be caused by a cold or allergies
  • Bacterial or viral infection, especially after you have a cold
  • Allergy attack
  • Changes in temperature, Air pressure
  • Pollution, Chemical fumes
  • Smoking
  • Swimming or diving
  • Excessive use of decongestant nasal sprays.

How to avoid -

  • Treating allergies and colds 
  • Moisten the air in your home. Humidifiers can better increase moisture in the air of your home. Air conditioners are also good way of stabilizing the temperature in either high or low weather. 
  • Avoid pollution 
  • Eliminate smoking habits and alcohol consumption
  • Reduce air travel 
  • Drink lots of water. Water increases the amount of moisture in the body
  • Avoid swimming, Refrain from diving 
  • Good hygiene, such as frequent hand wash with soap and water. 
  • Minimize consumption of dairy products 
  • Take many hot showers and inhale steam 
  • Avoid temperature extremes 
  • Get an influenza vaccine each year
  • Eat plenty of fruits and vegetables that are rich in antioxidants
  • Reduce stress
