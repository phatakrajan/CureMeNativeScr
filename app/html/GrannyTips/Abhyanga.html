'Abhyanga' or 'Abhyangha' -- the Ayurvedic oil massage -- is an integral part of the daily routine recommended by this healing system for overall health and well-being.

Precautions

  • It should be done on empty stomach.
  • The best time to have Abhyanga is in early morning. One should take shower / bath at least half an hour after Abhyanga. Always take hot shower/bath after Abhyanga.
  • Always use warm/ hot oil for Abhyanga.

Steps to be followed

  • On limbs (arms and legs), the movement should be towards heart.
  • Clockwise circular movement over abdomen. Over back it should be in same direction of musculature. i.e. starting from shoulder upto waist and away.
  • Over joints area and over face Abhyanga is done in circular strokes, soft strokes for face.
  • Always put some oil inside ears. Scalp and feet should be massaged very well with warm oil.

Though Abhyanga is described in Diwali in Indian calendar, it should be done every day during winter and at least once in a week in other seasons.

Sesame oil should be used during winter because of its higher oil content. While Coconut oil is ideal for seasons other than winter.

So treat yourself with Abhyanga not only in Diwali but regularly.
